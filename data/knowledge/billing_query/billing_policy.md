# Billing Policy — Quick Reference

## Refunds

- Refunds for duplicate charges are processed automatically once confirmed —
  no manager approval needed for amounts under $100.
- Refunds between $100–$500 require a team-lead note in the case (use the
  "Refund Approval" tag); no separate sign-off needed if the duplicate charge
  is clearly visible in the transaction log.
- Refunds over $500, or for non-duplicate disputes (e.g. "I didn't authorize
  this"), must be escalated to the Billing Disputes team. Do not promise a
  refund amount or timeline yourself — tell the customer the team will follow
  up within 3 business days.
- Refund timelines: 3-5 business days back to the original payment method.
  For bank transfers, it can take up to 10 business days — let the customer
  know this upfront so they don't call back early.

## Common charge types

- **Monthly subscription fee**: charged on the customer's billing anniversary
  date. If a customer cancelled but was still charged, check the cancellation
  date against the billing date — if cancellation was processed *after* the
  billing date, the charge is correct and the cancellation takes effect next
  cycle (explain this clearly, customers often think cancelling stops the
  current charge).
- **Late payment fee**: $10, applied if payment fails and isn't resolved
  within 5 days. This fee CAN be waived once per customer per 12 months as a
  goodwill gesture — check the account notes for prior waivers before
  offering this.
- **Overage charges**: usage above plan limits, billed at the rate shown on
  the customer's plan page. These are correct charges and not eligible for
  refund unless there's a proven billing system error.

## Identity verification

- Required fields: full name, date of birth, and account number OR the email
  on file.
- If a customer can't provide these, offer security questions (set up at
  account creation) as a fallback.
- Never read out, confirm, or discuss any account details — including
  whether an account exists — before identity is verified.

## Tone guidance

- If the customer is frustrated about a fee, acknowledge it before explaining
  policy: "I can see why that's frustrating — let me walk you through what
  happened and what we can do."
- Avoid saying "policy says" repeatedly — explain the *reason* for the policy
  where possible (e.g. "we wait 5 days before applying a late fee to give
  payments time to clear").
